Tempura

  1. Home
  2. Tempura

Ingredients

For the Tempura Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup ice-cold water
  • 1 egg

For the Tempura Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 sweet potato, sliced into thin rounds
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into thin rounds
  • 1 eggplant, sliced into thin rounds
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 1 cup dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • Grated daikon radish (optional)

Instructions

Preparing the Tempura Batter:

  • Prepare the Ice-Cold Water:

    • Ensure the water is ice-cold by adding ice cubes if necessary. This helps create a light, crispy batter.

  • Mix the Batter:

    • In a medium bowl, lightly beat the egg. Add the ice-cold water and mix well.
    • Add the flour to the egg mixture. Using chopsticks or a fork, stir the mixture just enough to combine. It’s okay if the batter is lumpy; overmixing will result in a dense batter.

Preparing the Tempura Ingredients:

  • Prepare the Shrimp:

    • Devein the shrimp and make a few small cuts on the underside to prevent them from curling during frying.

  • Prepare the Vegetables:

    • Slice the sweet potato, carrot, zucchini, and eggplant into thin rounds or julienned strips. Make sure the pieces are not too thick to ensure even cooking.

Preparing the Dipping Sauce:

  • Make the Sauce:
    • In a small saucepan, combine the dashi, soy sauce, and mirin. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, then remove from the heat. You can add grated daikon radish to the sauce for additional flavor if desired.

Frying the Tempura:

  • Heat the Oil:

    • Pour vegetable oil into a deep fryer or a large, deep pot to a depth of about 2 inches. Heat the oil to 350°F (175°C). Use a thermometer to maintain a consistent temperature.

  • Fry the Ingredients:

    • Lightly coat the shrimp and vegetable pieces in flour, shaking off any excess.
    • Dip each piece into the tempura batter, allowing any excess to drip off.
    • Carefully place the battered shrimp and vegetables into the hot oil. Fry in small batches to avoid overcrowding the pot.
    • Fry until the tempura is golden and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per piece. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked tempura to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

  • Serve Immediately:

    • Tempura is best enjoyed hot and fresh. Arrange the shrimp and vegetables on a serving platter and serve with the dipping sauce on the side.
