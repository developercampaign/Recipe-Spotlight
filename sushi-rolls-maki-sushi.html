Sushi Rolls (Maki Sushi)

  1. Home
  2. Sushi Rolls (Maki Sushi)

Ingredients

For the Sushi Rice:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/3 cup rice vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Fillings:

  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 8 oz fresh salmon or tuna, sliced into thin strips (or other preferred fillings such as crab sticks, cooked shrimp, or tofu)

For Assembling:

  • Nori (seaweed sheets)
  • Soy sauce, for dipping
  • Pickled ginger, for serving
  • Wasabi, for serving

Equipment:

  • Bamboo sushi mat
  • Plastic wrap
  • Sharp knife
  • Rice paddle or wooden spoon

Instructions

Preparing the Sushi Rice:

  • Rinse the Rice:

    • Place the sushi rice in a bowl and cover it with cold water. Swirl the rice around with your hand, then drain the water. Repeat this process 3-4 times until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and helps the rice cook properly.

  • Cook the Rice:

    • Combine the rinsed rice and 2 1/2 cups of water in a rice cooker. Cook according to the manufacturer's instructions. If you don't have a rice cooker, you can cook the rice on the stove. Bring the water and rice to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 18-20 minutes until the water is absorbed.

  • Season the Rice:

    • While the rice is cooking, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small saucepan. Heat over low heat, stirring until the sugar and salt dissolve. Do not boil.
    • Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl. Pour the vinegar mixture over the rice and gently fold it in with a rice paddle or wooden spoon. Be careful not to mash the rice. Let the rice cool to room temperature.

Preparing the Fillings:

  • Prepare the Cucumber:

    • Cut the cucumber into thin julienned strips, about 1/4 inch thick and 3-4 inches long.

  • Prepare the Avocado:

    • Cut the avocado in half, remove the pit, and slice the flesh into thin strips.

  • Prepare the Fish:

    • If using fresh salmon or tuna, slice it into thin strips, about 1/4 inch thick and 3-4 inches long.

Assembling the Sushi Rolls:

  • Set Up Your Workspace:

    • Place a bamboo sushi mat on a flat surface and cover it with plastic wrap. This prevents the rice from sticking to the mat.

  • Prepare the Nori:

    • Lay a sheet of nori on the plastic-covered sushi mat, shiny side down.

  • Spread the Rice:

    • Wet your hands with a mixture of water and a little rice vinegar to prevent sticking. Take a handful of sushi rice and spread it evenly over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top. The rice layer should be about 1/4 inch thick.

  • Add the Fillings:

    • Place a line of cucumber, avocado, and fish strips along the bottom edge of the rice. Be careful not to overfill.

  • Roll the Sushi:

    • Starting from the bottom edge, use the bamboo mat to carefully lift and roll the nori over the fillings, applying gentle pressure to keep the roll tight. Continue rolling until you reach the top border. Use a little water to moisten the top edge of the nori to seal the roll.

  • Slice the Roll:

    • Using a sharp knife, slice the roll into bite-sized pieces (about 6-8 pieces per roll). Wipe the knife with a damp cloth between cuts to ensure clean slices.
