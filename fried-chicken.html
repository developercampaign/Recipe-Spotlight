Fried Chicken

  1. Home
  2. Fried Chicken

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 1 whole chicken (cut into 8-10 pieces) or 3-4 pounds of your preferred chicken parts (legs, thighs, wings, breasts)
  • Buttermilk: 2 cups (for marinating)
  • Hot Sauce: 2 tablespoons (optional, for marinating)
  • Salt: 2 teaspoons
  • Black Pepper: 2 teaspoons
  • Garlic Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Onion Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Paprika: 2 teaspoons
  • Cayenne Pepper: 1/2 teaspoon (optional, for added heat)
  • All-Purpose Flour: 3 cups
  • Cornstarch: 1/4 cup (for extra crispiness)
  • Baking Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Vegetable Oil: For frying (about 4-6 cups, depending on the size of your frying pan)

Instructions

  • Prepare the buttermilk marinade: In a large bowl, combine the buttermilk, hot sauce (if using), 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 teaspoon of black pepper.
  • Marinate the chicken: Place the chicken pieces in the buttermilk mixture, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight for the best flavor and tenderness.
  • Mix the dry ingredients: In a large, shallow dish, combine the flour, cornstarch, baking powder, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper (if using), and the remaining 1 teaspoon each of salt and black pepper.
  • Coat the chicken pieces: Remove the chicken from the buttermilk marinade, allowing the excess to drip off. Dredge each piece in the flour mixture, pressing the coating onto the chicken to ensure it's well-covered. For an extra-crispy crust, you can double-dip by returning the chicken to the buttermilk and then back into the flour mixture.
  • Heat the oil: In a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). The oil should be deep enough to cover the chicken pieces by about halfway.
  • Fry in batches: Carefully place a few pieces of chicken into the hot oil, skin-side down. Do not overcrowd the pan. Fry the chicken for 12-15 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) and the coating is golden brown and crispy. Thicker pieces like thighs and breasts may take longer.
  • Drain the chicken: Remove the fried chicken from the oil and place it on a wire rack set over a baking sheet to drain any excess oil. This helps keep the chicken crispy.
  • Serve hot: Enjoy the fried chicken hot, with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or biscuits.
