Barbecue Ribs

  1. Home
  2. Barbecue Ribs

Ingredients

  • Pork Ribs: 2 racks (baby back ribs or St. Louis style)
  • Salt: 2 teaspoons
  • Black Pepper: 2 teaspoons
  • Paprika: 2 tablespoons
  • Brown Sugar: 2 tablespoons
  • Garlic Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Onion Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Cayenne Pepper (optional for heat): 1/2 teaspoon
  • Mustard: 2 tablespoons (to help the rub stick to the ribs)
  • Apple Cider Vinegar: 1/4 cup
  • Barbecue Sauce: 1-2 cups (your favorite brand or homemade)

Instructions

  • Remove the membrane: Flip the ribs over to the bone side. Using a knife, lift the thin membrane that covers the bones and pull it off. Removing this membrane helps the ribs become more tender.
  • Season the ribs: Mix the salt, black pepper, paprika, brown sugar, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper (if using) in a bowl to create the dry rub. Rub the mustard over both sides of the ribs to help the dry rub adhere. Then, generously apply the dry rub all over the ribs.
  • For grilling: Preheat your grill to low heat, around 225°F (110°C). If using a charcoal grill, set up a two-zone fire with coals on one side for indirect cooking.
  • For oven baking: Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C).
  • For grilling: Place the ribs on the grill over indirect heat (the cooler side), bone-side down. Close the lid and cook for 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat starts to pull away from the bones.
  • For oven baking: Place the ribs on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil. Cover the ribs with foil and bake for 2.5 to 3 hours.
  • After the first hour of cooking, you can start basting the ribs with a mixture of apple cider vinegar and a little water to keep them moist. Baste every hour if desired.
  • For extra tenderness, after 2 hours of cooking, you can wrap the ribs in aluminum foil with a little apple cider vinegar and return them to the grill or oven for the final hour of cooking.
  • In the last 30 minutes of cooking, brush the ribs generously with barbecue sauce. If grilling, you can move the ribs to direct heat to caramelize the sauce, but watch carefully to avoid burning.
  • Rest and serve: Once the ribs are tender and the sauce is sticky and caramelized, remove them from the grill or oven. Let the ribs rest for about 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with additional barbecue sauce on the side.
