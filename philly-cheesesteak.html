Philly Cheesesteak

  1. Home
  2. Philly Cheesesteak

Ingredients

  • Ribeye Steak: 1 pound, thinly sliced (you can ask your butcher to slice it thin, or partially freeze the steak and slice it yourself)
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Black Pepper: 1 teaspoon
  • Onion: 1 large, thinly sliced
  • Green Bell Pepper: 1 large, thinly sliced (optional)
  • Mushrooms: 1 cup, sliced (optional)
  • Provolone Cheese: 8 slices (or Cheese Whiz or American cheese, depending on your preference)
  • Hoagie Rolls: 4 soft rolls (preferably Amoroso rolls, if available)
  • Butter: 2 tablespoons (for toasting the rolls)
  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons (for cooking the steak and vegetables)

Instructions

  • Slice the steak: If not already done, slice the ribeye steak as thinly as possible. This allows the meat to cook quickly and evenly.
  • Slice the vegetables: Thinly slice the onion, bell pepper, and mushrooms (if using).
  • Heat the oil: In a large skillet or griddle over medium heat, add 1 tablespoon of olive oil.
  • Sauté the vegetables: Add the sliced onion, bell pepper, and mushrooms (if using) to the skillet. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are soft and slightly caramelized, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  • Heat more oil: In the same skillet or griddle, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil.
  • Cook the steak: Add the thinly sliced steak to the hot skillet in a single layer. Season with salt and black pepper. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, stirring and breaking up the meat with a spatula, until the steak is browned and cooked through.
  • Mix in the vegetables: Return the cooked vegetables to the skillet and mix them with the steak. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to combine the flavors.
  • Melt the cheese: Lay the provolone cheese slices over the steak and vegetable mixture. Let it melt for about 1 minute. Alternatively, if using Cheese Whiz, warm it separately and drizzle it over the steak mixture.
  • Toast the rolls: While the cheese is melting, slice the hoagie rolls open lengthwise, but don’t cut all the way through. Spread butter on the inside of each roll and toast them on the griddle or in the oven until lightly golden and crisp.
  • Fill the rolls: Once the cheese is melted, use a spatula to scoop the steak and vegetable mixture into the toasted hoagie rolls, ensuring each roll is filled evenly.
  • Serve hot: Serve the Philly Cheesesteaks immediately while they're hot and gooey.
