Buffalo Wings

  1. Home
  2. Buffalo Wings

Ingredients

  • Chicken Wings: 2 pounds (about 20 wings)
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Pepper: 1 teaspoon
  • Vegetable Oil: For frying (about 4 cups, depending on the size of your pot)

For the Buffalo Sauce:

  • Hot Sauce: 1/2 cup (Frank’s RedHot is a popular choice)
  • Unsalted Butter: 1/4 cup (melted)
  • Worcestershire Sauce: 1 tablespoon
  • Garlic Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Honey (optional for a touch of sweetness): 1-2 tablespoons

Instructions

  • Prepare the Chicken Wings: Separate the wings: If the wings are whole, cut them into three sections: drumette, flat, and tip. Discard the tips or save them for making stock.
  • Fry the Wings: Heat the oil: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). Use a thermometer to ensure the oil reaches and stays at the correct temperature.
  • Fry in batches: Fry the wings in small batches, about 10 minutes per batch, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Be careful not to overcrowd the pot, as this can lower the oil temperature.
  • Drain the wings: Remove the wings from the oil and let them drain on a wire rack over a baking sheet or on paper towels.
  • Mix the sauce: In a small saucepan, combine the hot sauce, melted butter, Worcestershire sauce, garlic powder, and honey (if using). Heat over medium-low heat, stirring until the ingredients are well combined and the sauce is smooth.
  • Coat the wings: Place the fried wings in a large mixing bowl. Pour the warm Buffalo sauce over the wings and toss until they are evenly coated.
  • Serve immediately: Serve the Buffalo Wings hot with celery sticks and blue cheese or ranch dressing on the side for dipping.
